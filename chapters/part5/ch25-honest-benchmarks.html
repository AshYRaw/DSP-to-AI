Part V — The Critical Lens
Chapter 25

Honest Benchmarks

Numbers without context are just numbers. This chapter scrutinizes the empirical claims made throughout Parts III–IV — parameter mismatches, bidirectional cheating, saturated benchmarks, and the contested expressiveness hierarchy. The goal: teach you to read ML papers with a critical eye.

25.1 Why Convolution Helps Mamba

Chapter 19 noted that Mamba includes a 1D convolution (kernel=4) before the SSM scan. The claim that “convolution provides local nonlinear feature extraction that Mamba’s linear recurrence lacks” conflates two distinct effects:

Nonlinearity Injection

The SSM core is linear in the state: ht = Atht-1 + Btxt. While the Mamba block is nonlinear (SiLU, gating, input-dependent params), the recurrence itself applies no nonlinear activation to the state. Conv + activation provides additional nonlinear mixing before the SSM.

Local Feature Extraction

Mamba’s kernel=4 provides ~40ms of local context (at 10ms stride). Conformer uses kernel=31 (~310ms). These are fundamentally different scales of local processing. The conv captures local coarticulation patterns — phone transitions, local edges — that the SSM processes sequentially.

The key insight: the Mamba block IS a nonlinear dynamical system at the block level. But the SSM core’s linearity means it needs external nonlinearity for hierarchical feature learning. Separating these concerns makes the architecture clearer.

25.2 Benchmark Comparisons Are Not Apples-to-Apples

When we said “ConMamba achieves 3.9%/10.3% WER vs Conformer’s 4.3%/11.3%” in Chapter 20, several critical caveats were missing:

IssueDetailImpact
Parameter mismatch ConMamba: 31.6M vs Conformer: 28.8M (10% more params) Improvement may be entirely from extra capacity
Bidirectional Mamba ConMamba uses bidir Mamba (forward + backward), doubling SSM compute Violates the causal/streaming advantage we claimed for Mamba
Benchmark saturation LibriSpeech is saturated at ~3–4% WER Small differences may not be statistically significant
The real story At 115M params, ConMamba ≈ Conformer Equivalence at scale is the informative data point

The proper comparison requires iso-parameter and iso-FLOP budgets, controlling for training data, augmentation, optimizer, and decoding method. The efficiency metrics (training time, memory, inference speed) are more robust than WER on saturated benchmarks.

25.3 The Expressiveness Hierarchy Is Contested

The Mamba-2 paper established a hierarchy: Selective SSMs > SSD > Linear Attention > Softmax Attention (in terms of representable functions). This is theoretically correct but practically misleading:

More expressive ≠ better

Softmax attention at the “bottom” doesn’t mean worst — it means most constrained. Constraints = regularization = better generalization with limited data. The bias-variance tradeoff applies to architectures too.

Both are limited to TC°

Merrill et al. (ICML 2024) and Sarrof et al. (NeurIPS 2024) showed that both Transformers and SSMs are limited to TC° (threshold circuits of constant depth) in practice. Neither is Turing-complete in finite precision.

Definition matters

If “expressiveness” means set of representable functions, the hierarchy holds. If it means sample efficiency at learning useful representations, the ordering may reverse — attention’s content-addressable memory may be more useful than raw approximation power.

Show the Math — Bias-Variance for Architectures

The expected test error decomposes as:

E[Ltest] = Bias² + Variance + Irreducible noise

More expressive architectures (Mamba-1 > Mamba-2 > Linear Attn > Softmax Attn) have lower bias but higher variance. With limited training data, higher-variance models overfit. With massive data, lower-bias models dominate. The crossover point where “more expressive beats more constrained” depends on dataset size.

25.4 Numbers Need Context

Samba-ASR’s impressive 1.17%/2.48% WER was cited alongside ConMamba’s ~3.9%/10.3% without adequate context:

16× data gap

Samba-ASR trains on 15,460 hours (LibriLight + VoxPopuli + more). ConMamba trains on 960 hours (LibriSpeech only). The fair comparison would be ConMamba at 15,460 hours.

External language model

At sub-2% WER, most systems use external LMs for decoding. This inflates results beyond what the acoustic model alone achieves.

“No attention” needs scrutiny

Samba-ASR’s “no attention” claim requires examining the cross-connection mechanism, which may be functionally similar to cross-attention through Mamba-based routing.

The Lesson for Reading Papers

Always check: (1) parameter count, (2) training data scale, (3) bidirectional vs causal, (4) decoding method, (5) confidence intervals. A result without these is a number without meaning. The best comparisons are iso-parameter, iso-FLOP, same-data, same-decoder.

Tool 25.2 Expressiveness vs Generalization
