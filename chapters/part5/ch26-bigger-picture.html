Chapter 26

The Bigger Picture

Does “one framework, two languages” overstate the connection? What can attention do that SSMs genuinely cannot? This chapter gives the honest summary of what holds, what breaks, and what the latest research (2025–2026) reveals about the convergence of these architectures.

26.1 Does “One Framework, Two Languages” Overstate the Connection?

The concluding claim of Part III — that DSP and modern AI “share the same mathematics” — risks conflating structural isomorphisms with functional equivalences. The state-space recurrence hk = Āhk-1 + &Bmacr;uk appears in:

SystemRole of hWhat We Care About
IIR Filter Means to compute y = Cx The output (filtered signal)
Kalman Filter The goal (state estimate) The state itself
Mamba Information bottleneck Compressed history for downstream tasks

The mathematical correspondence is exact. The semantic correspondence is approximate. Same equation, different meaning. Acknowledging this distinction is intellectual honesty — and it’s what separates understanding from pattern matching.

26.2 What Attention Can Do That SSMs Can’t

Parts III–IV focused on Mamba’s advantages. Here are attention’s unique strengths — updated with the latest research:

In-Context Learning = Adaptive Filtering!

Transformers implement batch gradient descent in their forward pass (Akyurek et al., 2023). Mamba does online gradient descent (= LMS adaptive filter!). This is actually the strongest DSP analogy: Mamba’s ICL is literally the LMS algorithm from Ch 8.

Compositionality & Variable Binding

Attention’s content-addressable memory supports variable binding — associating arbitrary keys with arbitrary values. SSMs’ compressed state struggles with compositional tasks. “Illusion of State” (ICML 2024) confirmed: SSM state doesn’t help with compositional reasoning.

Both Limited to TC°

The old claim that “Transformers are Turing-complete but SSMs are not” is outdated. Merrill et al. (ICML 2024) showed both are limited to TC° in practice. The real difference is in how efficiently they use their limited computational class.

The Real Value of the DSP Lens

DSP doesn’t give us exact equivalences for everything. What it gives is: (1) intuition for why architectures work, (2) initialization strategies (HiPPO, Butterworth poles), (3) stability guarantees (Lyapunov analysis), and (4) a vocabulary for reasoning about memory, filtering, and adaptation. That’s not everything, but it’s a lot.

26.4 Updated Landscape (2025–2026)

DevelopmentWhat It MeansSource
Mamba-2 SSD SSM with scalar A = masked attention via semiseparable matrices. The theoretical bridge is now exact for this subclass. Dao & Gu, ICML 2024
Mamba-3 Complex-valued dynamics produce oscillatory modes = resonant filter poles. Direct connection to DSP resonators. OpenReview 2025
Hidden Attention Mamba’s SSM can be rewritten as implicit attention matrices — bridging interpretability between paradigms. Ali et al., ACL 2025
Lyapunov Stability Mamba proven stable by construction: all Lyapunov exponents non-positive. Validates DSP stability analysis. ICLR 2025 workshop
Hybrid Convergence Jamba (1:7), Griffin (local attention), Zamba (shared attention), Bamba (enterprise) — all converging on hybrid designs. Multiple 2024–2025

The field is converging: SSMs and attention are not rivals but complementary tools. The DSP lens predicted this — FIR and IIR filters have been cascaded for 70 years because each solves different problems. The hybrid future is the DSP past.
