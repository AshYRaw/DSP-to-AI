Part V — The Critical Lens
Chapter 27

The Experimental Frontier

Chapters 24–26 identified where the DSP↔AI analogies hold and where they break. This final chapter proposes five experimental programs to rigorously test every claim. These are not thought experiments — they’re research blueprints for the next generation of DSP-informed AI.

27.1 Program A: The Filter Probe

Goal: Reverse-engineer the frequency selectivity of trained SSM channels. If the IIR analogy is deep, trained Mamba channels should learn interpretable filter characteristics.

Step 1: Extract

Train Mamba on speech → extract learned (A, B, C) parameters per channel → compute frozen Hi(z) for each channel.

Step 2: Classify

Classify channels: low-pass (pole near z=1), band-pass (complex poles near unit circle), high-pass (pole near z=−1), all-pass (poles = conjugate of zeros).

Step 3: Validate

Do band-pass channels have formant-like center frequencies? Do deeper layers have higher Q (narrower bandwidth)? If yes, Mamba IS discovering filter bank structure.

27.2 Program B: Mamba-Vocoder

Hypothesis: If Mamba IS an IIR filter bank, driving trained SSM channels with white noise or impulse trains should produce speech-like formant structure — exactly like a classical LP vocoder.

The Radical Test

Extract the learned SSM from a speech-trained Mamba. Drive each channel with excitation signals (white noise for unvoiced, impulse trains for voiced). If the output has formant-like spectral peaks, Mamba has literally learned a parametric speech model. If not, the IIR analogy, while mathematically correct, doesn’t capture the functional role.

27.3 Program C: DSP-Guided Architecture Search

Goal: Use autocorrelation analysis R(τ) to predict the optimal attention-to-Mamba ratio without exhaustive search.

The key insight: compute R(τ) of input features, decompose into local (IIR-capturable) and long-range (needs attention) components. The ratio of long-range energy η = E[Rlong] / E[Rtotal] should predict the optimal architecture mix.

27.4 Program D: Stability Analysis of Trained Models

Goal: Verify that Mamba maintains stability in practice and characterize its stability margins with real speech input.

What to Measure

1. Lyapunov exponents Λi (must be negative for stability).
2. Max eigenvalue magnitude ρ(t) over time.
3. How close do poles get to the unit circle? (Stability margin)

Recent Confirmation

ICLR 2025 workshop paper proved Mamba’s Lyapunov exponents are non-positive by construction. Since Re(ai) < 0 and Δt > 0, |λi(t)| < 1 always. The DSP stability analysis from Chapter 6 applies directly.

Prediction: Well-trained Mamba models operate with stability margins of 0.05–0.15 (poles at |λ| = 0.85–0.95), with brief excursions closer to 1.0 during semantically important moments (onsets, boundaries).

27.5 Program E: DSP-Mamba — The Ultimate Test

The big question: Do DSP priors help, or does end-to-end learning find better solutions? Build a Mamba variant where every design choice is DSP-motivated:

ComponentDSP-Mamba DesignStandard Mamba
Initialization HiPPO (1/3) + Butterworth (1/3) + Bessel (1/3) HiPPO only
Pre-SSM conv kernel=15 (150ms coarticulation span) kernel=4
Stability r·exp(jθ) with r = sigmoid(α) Softplus on Δ
Gating Explicit bandwidth control: BWt = σ(Linear(xt))·BWmax SiLU gating
Output Cepstral projection (DCT on SSM outputs) Linear projection

Why This Matters

If DSP-Mamba outperforms standard Mamba, it proves that 70 years of filter design wisdom can directly improve modern AI. If it doesn’t, it proves that end-to-end learning discovers representations that surpass hand-designed DSP priors. Either answer is valuable — and either answer is a victory for understanding.
