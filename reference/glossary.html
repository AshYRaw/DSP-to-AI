DSP Foundations

Signalx[n] or x(t)
DSP
A function that carries information, mapping time (or space) to amplitude. Continuous signals x(t) exist for all time; discrete-time signals x[n] exist only at integer indices after sampling.
AI Input tensor / token sequence
Ch 1
Sampling & Nyquistfs, fN
DSP
Converting a continuous signal to discrete by measuring at regular intervals. The Nyquist rate (2× the highest frequency) is the minimum sampling rate to avoid aliasing — where high frequencies masquerade as low ones.
Ch 1
LTI SystemLinear Time-Invariant
DSP
A system where scaling the input scales the output (linearity) and delaying the input delays the output (time-invariance). Completely characterized by its impulse response h[n]. The backbone of classical DSP.
AI Fixed-weight layer (no input-dependent params)
Ch 2
Convolution(x * h)[n]
DSPMATH
Slide, multiply, sum. The fundamental operation of DSP: y[n] = ∑ x[k] · h[n−k]. Applies a kernel (impulse response) to every position in the input. Produces the output of any LTI system.
AI Conv layer / matrix multiplication
Ch 3
Fourier TransformDFT / FFT
DSPMATH
Decomposes any signal into a sum of sinusoids at different frequencies. Converts from time domain to frequency domain. The FFT computes it in O(N log N). Convolution in time becomes multiplication in frequency.
AI Learned basis / feature decomposition
Ch 4
Z-TransformX(z), H(z)
DSPMATH
Extends Fourier to the full complex plane z = rejω. The delay operator z−1 turns difference equations into polynomials. The transfer function H(z) = Y(z)/X(z) fully describes any LTI system as a ratio of polynomials.
Ch 5
Poles & Zerosz-plane
DSPMATH
Poles are frequencies where the system has infinite gain (roots of the denominator). Zeros are frequencies of zero gain (roots of the numerator). Poles inside the unit circle ⇒ stable system. Pole angle = resonant frequency; pole radius = decay rate.
AI Eigenvalues of state matrix A
Ch 6
FIR FilterFinite Impulse Response
DSP
Output depends only on a finite window of past inputs: y[n] = ∑ bk x[n−k]. Always stable, can have linear phase, but needs many taps for sharp cutoffs. Computation: O(M) per sample where M = number of taps.
AI Attention (explicit, finite-window memory)
Ch 7, Ch 20
IIR FilterInfinite Impulse Response
DSP
Uses feedback (recursion): output depends on past inputs and past outputs. Can approximate sharp responses with few coefficients, but can be unstable if poles leave the unit circle. Memory is compressed into a state vector, not stored explicitly.
AI SSM / Mamba (recursive state, compressed memory)
Ch 7, Ch 20
Filter Bankparallel channels
DSP
An array of filters applied in parallel, each tuned to a different frequency band. Decomposes a signal into sub-bands for independent processing. Used in audio codecs, wavelets, and speech processing.
AI Multi-head attention / multi-channel SSM
Ch 8
Adaptive FilterLMS algorithm
DSP
A filter whose coefficients update in real-time to minimize error. The LMS algorithm updates weights via: w[n+1] = w[n] + μ · e[n] · x[n], where μ is the step size. Used for noise cancellation, echo removal, and equalization.
AI Training / SGD / gradient descent
Ch 8
Matched Filter
DSP
A filter whose impulse response is the time-reversed conjugate of the signal you want to detect. Maximizes signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) at the output. Its output is the cross-correlation between input and template.
AI Dot-product similarity / neuron activation
Ch 17
Frequency ResponseH(ejω)
DSP
How a system responds to each frequency: evaluate the transfer function on the unit circle. Has magnitude (how much each frequency is amplified/attenuated) and phase (how much each frequency is delayed).
AI Feature selectivity pattern
Ch 4, Ch 6
Window FunctionsHann, Hamming, Blackman
DSP
Smooth tapering functions applied to finite-length signals to reduce spectral leakage. Trade off between main-lobe width (frequency resolution) and side-lobe level (leakage suppression). Used in FIR design and spectral analysis.
Ch 7
DiscretizationZOH, Bilinear
DSP
Converting a continuous-time system to discrete-time. Zero-Order Hold (ZOH) assumes input is constant between samples. Bilinear (Tustin) maps the entire s-plane into the z-plane preserving stability. Critical for SSMs.
AI SSM discretization step (learnable Δ)
Ch 18
Impulse Responseh[n]
DSP
The output of an LTI system when the input is a unit impulse δ[n]. Completely characterizes the system — the output for any input is the convolution of that input with h[n]. FIR systems have finite h[n]; IIR systems have infinitely long h[n] that decays.
AI Network weights / convolution kernel
Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 16
Transfer FunctionH(z)
DSPMATH
The Z-transform of the impulse response: H(z) = Y(z)/X(z). A ratio of polynomials whose roots are the poles and zeros. Evaluating H(z) on the unit circle gives the frequency response. The complete algebraic description of any LTI system.
AI SSM kernel in Z-domain / layer transfer characteristic
Ch 5, Ch 18
Causality
DSP
A system is causal if its output at time n depends only on present and past inputs, not future ones. Required for real-time processing. In AI: causal attention masks future tokens; SSM recurrence is inherently causal (state only depends on past).
AI Causal attention mask / autoregressive generation
Ch 2, Ch 14

AI Foundations

NeuronPerceptron
AI
The fundamental unit: y = σ(wTx + b). Computes a weighted sum of inputs (dot product), adds bias, applies a nonlinear activation. The McCulloch-Pitts model (1943), Rosenblatt's Perceptron (1958).
DSP Matched filter (dot product = correlation)
Ch 9
Activation FunctionsReLU, Sigmoid, GELU
AI
Nonlinear functions applied after the weighted sum. Sigmoid: smooth 0-to-1 squash. ReLU: max(0, x), simple and fast. GELU: smooth ReLU used in transformers. Without nonlinearity, stacked layers collapse to a single linear operation.
Ch 9
Backpropagationchain rule
AIMATH
The algorithm for computing gradients efficiently via the chain rule. Flows error backwards through the network, computing ∂L/∂w for every weight. Enables gradient descent to update parameters to minimize loss.
DSP LMS error feedback / Wiener solution gradient
Ch 10
SGD & OptimizersAdam, learning rate
AI
Stochastic Gradient Descent: update weights by subtracting gradient × learning rate. Adam adds momentum and adaptive per-parameter rates. The learning rate controls step size — too large diverges, too small stalls.
DSP LMS step size μ
Ch 11
RNNRecurrent Neural Network
AI
Processes sequences by maintaining a hidden state that updates at each timestep: ht = f(ht-1, xt). Suffers from vanishing gradients for long sequences. LSTM and GRU add gating to control information flow.
DSP IIR filter (recursive state update)
Ch 12
EmbeddingWord2Vec, learned basis
AI
Maps discrete tokens (words, pixels) into a continuous vector space where geometric distance reflects semantic similarity. Replaces sparse one-hot encoding with dense learned representations. "King − Man + Woman ≈ Queen."
DSP Learned basis functions / signal representation
Ch 13
AttentionQ, K, V
AI
Soft database lookup. Computes relevance scores between all pairs of tokens: Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QKT/√d) · V. Multi-head: run multiple attention in parallel on different projections. Complexity: O(T²) in sequence length.
DSP Time-varying FIR filter / matched filter bank
Ch 14
Transformer
AI
Architecture built entirely from attention + feed-forward layers (Vaswani et al., 2017). Each block: self-attention → add & norm → FFN → add & norm. Processes all tokens in parallel, enabling massive-scale training. Foundation of GPT, BERT, and modern LLMs.
Ch 15
KV-Cache
AI
During autoregressive generation, attention stores all past keys and values to avoid recomputation. Provides perfect, lossless recall of any previous token, but memory grows linearly with sequence length: O(T · d) per layer.
DSP Full signal buffer (explicit memory)
Ch 20
Softmax
AI
Converts a vector of raw scores into a probability distribution: softmax(zi) = ezi / ∑ ezj. Used in attention weights and classification outputs. Temperature controls sharpness: low T → peaky, high T → uniform.
Ch 14
Multi-Head Attention
AI
Runs h independent attention operations in parallel, each with its own Q, K, V projections into a d/h-dimensional subspace. Different heads learn to attend to different types of relationships (syntactic, semantic, positional). Outputs are concatenated and projected.
DSP Parallel filter bank (each channel tuned differently)
Ch 14
Positional Encodingsinusoidal, RoPE
AI
Injects position information into the attention mechanism, which is otherwise order-agnostic. The original Transformer uses sinusoidal encodings (literal Fourier basis functions). Modern models use Rotary Position Embeddings (RoPE) that encode relative positions.
DSP Fourier basis functions / window position tagging
Ch 15
Residual Connectionskip connection
AI
Adds the input directly to the output of a layer: y = F(x) + x. Allows gradients to flow unchanged through the identity path, preventing vanishing gradients in deep networks. Used in every Transformer block and Mamba block. The layer only needs to learn the residual F(x).
DSP Feedforward path / D matrix in SSM
Ch 15, Ch 19
LayerNorm / RMSNorm
AI
LayerNorm normalizes activations to zero mean and unit variance across the feature dimension. RMSNorm (used in Mamba, Llama) simplifies by only dividing by the RMS, skipping the mean subtraction. Stabilizes training by keeping activations in a well-conditioned range.
Ch 15, Ch 19
GELU / SiLUSwish
AI
GELU (x · Φ(x)): smooth approximation to ReLU used in BERT and GPT. SiLU/Swish (x · σ(x)): self-gated activation used in Mamba blocks. Both are smooth, non-monotonic near zero, and avoid the "dead neuron" problem of ReLU.
Ch 9, Ch 19
Grouped-Query AttentionGQA
AI
Shares K and V projections across groups of query heads, reducing KV-cache size by the group factor. Used in Llama-2/3 and hybrid architectures. A middle ground between multi-head attention (separate KV per head) and multi-query attention (one shared KV).
Ch 22
Induction Head
AI
A two-head circuit in Transformers that implements pattern completion: if "A B ... A" appeared, predict "B" next. The core mechanism for in-context learning. SSMs struggle with this because it requires exact retrieval from history — a key motivation for hybrid architectures.
Ch 20, Ch 22

The Bridge — SSMs, Mamba & Duality

State-Space Model (SSM)x' = Ax + Bu, y = Cx
BRIDGEMATH
A model that maps input to output through a latent state vector. Defined by matrices A (state dynamics), B (input coupling), C (output readout), D (skip). The continuous form is discretized with timestep Δ for sequence processing.
DSP State-space realization of a transfer function
AI Structured sequence model layer
Ch 18
HiPPOHigh-Order Polynomial Projection Operator
BRIDGEMATH
A principled initialization for the A matrix that optimally compresses input history into a fixed-size state using Legendre polynomial projections. Solves the "how to remember long sequences in finite memory" problem. Foundation of S4 and all subsequent SSMs.
Ch 18, Ch 21
S4Structured State Spaces for Sequences
BRIDGE
The first practical SSM architecture (Gu et al., ICLR 2022). Uses DPLR (diagonal plus low-rank) parameterization of A to enable efficient O(T log T) training via FFT. Exploits conv-recurrence duality: train as convolution, infer as recurrence.
DSP Fixed IIR filter bank (parallel first-order sections)
Ch 18, Ch 21
Conv-Recurrence Duality
BRIDGE
Any linear recurrence xt = Axt-1 + But can be unrolled into a convolution y = K * u where K is the impulse response. Training: use convolution (parallel, O(T log T) via FFT). Inference: use recurrence (O(1) per step, streaming).
DSP IIR filter ↔ truncated FIR kernel
Ch 18
MambaSelective SSM / S6
BRIDGE
Makes SSM parameters (Δ, B, C) input-dependent, breaking LTI to become LTV. The selection mechanism lets the model decide what to remember/forget per token. Uses hardware-aware parallel scan for efficient GPU execution. O(T) complexity with content-aware processing.
DSP Adaptive IIR filter bank (poles move per timestep)
Ch 19
Selective Scanparallel associative scan
BRIDGEMATH
The core algorithm of Mamba. Exploits the associativity of the recurrence (A, Bu) tuples to parallelize what looks sequential. Computed in O(T) work and O(log T) depth on GPU using a binary tree reduction, entirely in SRAM to minimize HBM transfers.
Ch 19
Delta (Δ)learnable timestep
BRIDGEMATH
The master control in Mamba. Determines how much the state evolves per token. Large Δ → fast decay, focus on current input (reset). Small Δ → slow decay, retain history (remember). Computed as Δ = softplus(Linear(x)), making it input-dependent.
DSP Pole radius / sampling period / gate opening
Ch 19
Mamba-2 / SSDStructured State Space Duality
BRIDGE
Proves that the selective SSM computation is mathematically equivalent to a form of structured masked attention where the mask is a semiseparable matrix. Unifies the two paradigms: SSM ≡ structured attention. Enables 2–8× faster implementation using tensor cores.
Ch 20, Ch 21
LTV SystemLinear Time-Varying
BRIDGE
A system whose parameters change over time (or depend on input). Unlike LTI systems, an LTV system's response to the same input can differ depending on when or what triggers it. Mamba is LTV because Δ, B, C depend on the input token.
DSP Adaptive / time-varying filter
AI Input-dependent gating (Mamba, attention)
Ch 19
State Vectorcompressed memory
BRIDGE
A fixed-size vector x ∈ RN that summarizes all past input through recursive updates. Unlike the KV-cache, it doesn't grow with sequence length, but information is lossy — compressed into N dimensions regardless of how long the history is.
DSP IIR filter state registers
AI RNN hidden state / SSM latent state
Ch 18, Ch 20
DPLRDiagonal Plus Low-Rank
BRIDGEMATH
The key parameterization that made S4 practical. Decomposes the HiPPO A matrix as A = Λ + PQ* (diagonal + low-rank correction). Enables O(N) kernel computation via Cauchy kernel evaluation — equivalent to computing the frequency response via partial fractions.
DSP Partial fraction expansion of H(z)
Ch 18, Ch 21
Gated Linear RecurrenceGLR
BRIDGEAI
A simplified form of SSM using a real-valued diagonal recurrence with input-dependent gating: xt = αt · xt-1 + (1−αt) · ut. Used in Griffin and Hawk. Simpler than Mamba’s complex-valued diagonal, but effective when paired with local attention.
DSP First-order adaptive IIR with real pole
Ch 21, Ch 22

The Frontier — Hybrids & Evolution

Hybrid ArchitectureJamba, Griffin, Bamba
BRIDGEAI
Interleaves SSM layers (for efficiency and long-range) with attention layers (for retrieval and reasoning). Design choices: ratio (how many attention layers), placement (where), type (sliding-window vs full attention), SSM variant.
DSP FIR + IIR cascade (best of both worlds)
Ch 22
JambaAI21, 2024
AI
First production-scale hybrid: Mamba + Attention at 1:7 ratio (1 attention layer per 7 SSM layers), combined with Mixture of Experts (MoE). 52B parameters, 256K context. Demonstrated that very few attention layers are sufficient for strong retrieval.
Ch 22
GriffinDeepMind, 2024
AI
DeepMind's hybrid combining Real Gated Linear Recurrence (a simplified SSM) with local sliding-window attention. Part of the Hawk/Griffin family. Demonstrates that even simple linear recurrences, when combined with attention, achieve strong results.
Ch 22
Sliding-Window Attentionlocal attention
AI
Restricts attention to a fixed-size local window W around each token instead of all T past tokens. Reduces complexity from O(T²) to O(T·W). Used in hybrid architectures where SSM layers handle long-range and attention handles local precision.
DSP Windowed FIR filter (finite-length kernel)
Ch 22
Edge Deployment
AI
Running models on resource-constrained devices (mobile, IoT, embedded). SSM-heavy hybrids excel here due to constant memory (no KV-cache growth), O(1) per-step inference, and natural streaming capability. Models like XAMBA and eMamba target this space.
Ch 22, Ch 21
In-Context Retrievalassociative recall
BRIDGE
The ability to look up and copy specific information from earlier in the context. Attention excels here (direct access via KV-cache). SSMs struggle because state compression is lossy. This is the primary reason hybrids include attention layers.
Ch 20, Ch 22
Mixture of ExpertsMoE
AI
Replaces a single FFN with N parallel expert networks, routing each token to the top-k experts via a learned gating function. Total parameters are large but active parameters per token are small (sparse). Used in Jamba (Mamba + Attention + MoE) for efficiency at scale.
Ch 22
BambaIBM, 2024
AI
IBM's open-source 9B hybrid: 29 Mamba-2 layers + 3 attention layers (~9% attention). Demonstrates that minimal attention is sufficient at scale. Trained on 2T tokens with strong performance on reasoning and long-context tasks.
Ch 22, Ch 21
HyenaICML 2023
AIBRIDGE
Uses implicit long convolutions — data-driven convolution kernels parameterized by a small neural network instead of explicit weights. Sub-quadratic in sequence length. An important stepping stone between S4's fixed kernels and Mamba's fully input-dependent approach.
DSP Learned FIR kernel via implicit parameterization
Ch 21
RWKVReceptance Weighted Key Value
AIBRIDGE
A fully open-source architecture combining linear attention with RNN-like recurrence. Achieves O(T) training and O(1) inference like Mamba, but via a different mechanism (WKV operator). Widely deployed; versions up to 14B. Bridges the linear attention and SSM communities.
Ch 21
RetNetMicrosoft, 2023
AIBRIDGE
Replaces softmax attention with a retention mechanism that decays with distance: R = (QKT ⊙ D) V, where D is an exponential decay mask. Supports three modes: parallel (training), recurrent (inference), and chunkwise (hybrid). Bridges linear attention and SSMs.
DSP Exponentially-windowed FIR filter
Ch 21

Mathematical Foundations

Eigenvaluesspectral decomposition
MATH
Scalars λ where A·v = λ·v. Determine the natural modes of a system. In SSMs, the eigenvalues of A control decay rates and oscillation frequencies. |λ| < 1 ⇒ decaying mode (stable). |λ| = 1 ⇒ sustained. |λ| > 1 ⇒ unstable.
DSP Poles of the transfer function
Ch 6, Ch 18
StabilityBIBO, gradient flow
MATH
BIBO stability: bounded input ⇒ bounded output. In DSP: all poles inside the unit circle. In AI: no exploding gradients / activations. The same mathematical condition governs both — eigenvalues of the recurrence matrix must have magnitude < 1.
DSP Poles inside unit circle
AI No exploding gradients
Ch 6, Ch 16
Linear Transformationmatrix multiply
MATH
A function f where f(ax + by) = a·f(x) + b·f(y). Represented as matrix multiplication y = Wx. The dot product wTx is a single row of this operation — the neuron, the matched filter, the correlation computation.
Ch 9, Ch 16
Toeplitz Matrixconvolution as matrix multiply
MATH
A matrix where each descending diagonal has the same value. Convolution y = h * x is equivalent to y = T·x where T is a Toeplitz matrix built from h. This identity bridges DSP's convolution with AI's matrix multiplication.
Ch 16
Semiseparable Matrix
MATH
A matrix where every submatrix below the diagonal has rank at most N (the state dimension). The Mamba-2 / SSD result: the SSM output matrix is semiseparable, and this is exactly the structure of causal attention with a specific mask. The mathematical proof that SSMs ≡ structured attention.
Ch 20, Ch 21
Computational ComplexityO(n²) vs O(n)
MATH
How resource usage scales with input size. Attention: O(T²·d) — every token attends to every other. Mamba: O(T·d·N) — linear in sequence length. At T=131K, this means 131,000× less compute for the sequence-mixing step.
Ch 20
Associativityparallel prefix scan
MATHDSP
The property (a ⊕ b) ⊕ c = a ⊕ (b ⊕ c). Critical for parallelizing sequential operations. In Mamba, the recurrence tuples (At, Btut) form a monoid under composition, enabling O(log T) depth parallel scan on GPU.
Ch 19
Vanishing / Exploding Gradients
MATH
When gradients shrink to zero (vanishing) or grow unboundedly (exploding) through deep networks or long sequences. Caused by repeated multiplication by matrices with eigenvalues |λ| ≠ 1. HiPPO/SSMs address this by carefully initializing A so eigenvalues stay near the stability boundary.
DSP Poles too far inside (overdamped) or outside (unstable) unit circle
Ch 10, Ch 16
Softpluslog(1 + ex)
MATHBRIDGE
A smooth approximation to ReLU that always produces positive outputs: softplus(x) = log(1 + ex). Used in Mamba to compute Δ = softplus(Linear(x)), ensuring the timestep is always positive (required for valid discretization).
Ch 19
Cauchy KernelS4 kernel computation
MATHBRIDGE
The function 1/(ω − λ) that appears when evaluating H(z) via partial fractions at points on the unit circle. S4 exploits this to compute the SSM convolution kernel in O(N) per frequency. Mathematically identical to evaluating the frequency response of a parallel bank of first-order IIR sections.
DSP Frequency response evaluation via partial fractions
